# Measuring the Impact of AI Tools on Students' Productivity

A primary quantitative research study examining how AI tool usage influences academic productivity across three core dimensions: Time Efficiency, Error Reduction, and Academic Task Automation — conducted among 388 students in North Gujarat.

---

## 🎯 Research Objective

Most discussions around AI in education focus on ethics, adoption trends, or system design. This study shifts the focus to something more concrete: **does AI actually make students more productive, and if so, through which mechanisms?**

The study operationalizes student academic productivity into four measurable constructs and tests their relationship using multiple linear regression.

## 🔬 Research Model

**Dependent Variable**
- SAP — Students' Academic Productivity

**Independent Variables**
- TE — Time Efficiency
- ER — Error Reduction
- ATA — Academic Task Automation
- PLS — Personalized Learning Support

**Regression Equation:**
`SAP = 0.490 + 0.121(TE) + 0.316(ER) + 0.060(ATA) + 0.382(PLS)`

| Component | Details |

---

## 💡 Key Findings

- All four hypotheses supported at statistically significant levels (p < 0.05)
- **Personalized Learning Support** is the strongest driver of academic productivity — AI's value is highest when it functions as an adaptive learning partner, not just a task-execution tool
- **Time Efficiency** had the highest perceived mean (4.19) — time saving is the most universally recognized AI benefit among students
- **Academic Task Automation** was the weakest construct (mean 3.72) — students perceive AI automation as still developing for complex academic tasks
- The model explains **56.2% of variance** in academic productivity — strong explanatory power for a social science study
